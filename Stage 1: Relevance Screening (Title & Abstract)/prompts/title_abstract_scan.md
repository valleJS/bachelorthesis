## Screening Instructions

Apply the following criteria in strict order. This order is mandatory and must not
be reversed.

### Step 1 - Check Inclusion Criteria
Determine whether the paper meets ANY ONE of the inclusion criteria below.
If none apply, classify as NOT_RELEVANT immediately. Do not proceed to Step 2.

### Step 2 - Check Exclusion Criteria
Only reached if at least one inclusion criterion is met.
Determine whether ANY ONE exclusion criterion applies.
If any exclusion criterion applies, classify as NOT_RELEVANT.
Exclusion criteria override inclusion criteria without exception.

### Step 3 - Special Override: I4 Review and Theory Papers
If the paper meets I4 (foundational or review contribution to signalling theory),
it is NEVER excluded on the basis of E3 (non-technology-focused signals only).
Review and theory papers are included for their theoretical contribution regardless
of signal type. All other exclusion criteria still apply to I4 papers.

### Step 4 - Classify
- RELEVANT: at least one inclusion criterion met AND no exclusion criterion applies
  (accounting for the I4 override in Step 3).
- NOT_RELEVANT: no inclusion criterion met, OR at least one exclusion criterion
  applies (subject to the I4 override).
- UNSURE: at least one inclusion criterion appears to be met AND no exclusion
  criterion can be clearly confirmed or ruled out - but ONLY because the title
  and abstract do not contain sufficient information to make a confident
  determination. UNSURE is not a catch-all for borderline cases; it is reserved
  strictly for cases where the abstract is too vague or incomplete to apply the
  hierarchy. These papers will be screened at full-text level.

## Inclusion Criteria (any ONE makes a paper relevant)

**I1 - Core: Technology-related signalling in entrepreneurial finance**
Paper examines signalling mechanisms (explicit or implicit) used by ventures to
communicate technological capability, technological quality, feasibility,
appropriability, or innovation to early-stage investors (VC, angel, equity
crowdfunding, ICO/STO).

**I2 - Technology-related moderators of signalling effects**
Paper examines how characteristics of the technology, innovation, industry, or
sector moderate the effectiveness of signals in investor decisions - even if
"technology type" is not the main variable. This includes studies that incidentally
report sectoral or industry variation in signal effects (e.g., patents in biotech
vs. electronics).

**I3 - Technology typologies / taxonomies usable for the framework**
Paper proposes, tests, or applies a typology or taxonomy of technologies, digital
artifacts, AI systems, or IT systems that uses dimensions potentially relevant to
the framework (logic/interpretability, autonomy, learning, behaviour/output,
agency, generativity, etc.).

**I4 - Theoretical foundations of signalling**
Paper is a foundational or review contribution to signalling theory (Spence
tradition, Connelly et al. review, subsequent syntheses), OR a review of
signalling in entrepreneurship/VC specifically.

**I5 - VC decision-making under technological uncertainty**
Paper empirically or theoretically examines how VCs evaluate ventures under
technological uncertainty, due diligence processes for technology-intensive
ventures, or information problems specific to deep tech, science-based, or
frontier-technology ventures.

**I6 - Funding outcomes for specific technology types**
Paper reports funding success (probability, valuation, round size, investor type,
exit) for ventures in specific technology categories (AI, biotech, blockchain,
CRISPR, robotics, SaaS, deep tech) where the findings could inform
typology-level theorising.

## Exclusion Criteria (any ONE justifies exclusion, subject to Step 3 override)

**E1 - Wrong financing context**
Paper focuses primarily on bank debt, trade credit, corporate bond issuance, or
public equity markets beyond the IPO signalling point. Exclude unless the
signalling mechanism itself transfers meaningfully to early-stage equity finance.
Boundary case: IPO signalling papers are INCLUDED if they theorise
technology-related signals (e.g., patent signalling at IPO). They are EXCLUDED
if they focus on underpricing, underwriter effects, or post-IPO performance
without technology signalling content.

**E2 - No signalling or information-asymmetry framing**
Paper examines funding success but does not engage with signalling theory,
information asymmetry, or equivalent adverse-selection framing. Performance
studies, resource-based view studies, or strategic management studies without
an information problem are excluded.
Boundary case: Papers using "legitimacy theory" or "impression management"
INCLUDED if they address information asymmetry mechanisms; otherwise excluded.

**E3 - Non-technology-focused signals only**
Paper examines signalling but the signals of interest are purely non-technological
(e.g., entrepreneurial passion, gender, rhetoric, narrative style, pitch quality)
and the paper does not speak to how technology characteristics moderate signal
effects.
Boundary case: Human capital signals (founder technical background) are INCLUDED
because they interact with technology type in relevant ways; pure
behavioural/interpersonal signals are EXCLUDED.
Note: E3 does not apply to papers meeting I4. See Step 3.

**E4 - Technology typology without relevance to signalling or capability**
Paper classifies technologies but the dimensions are purely technical, functional,
or architectural (e.g., cloud service delivery models, software testing types,
network topologies) without engaging any dimension that plausibly affects what
can be signalled to an external observer.

**E5 - Wrong unit of analysis**
Paper studies established firms, large corporations, public-sector adoption, or
consumer behaviour rather than entrepreneurial ventures and their investors.
Corporate-venture-capital papers are INCLUDED; CVC-as-acquirer papers without
a startup-signalling angle are EXCLUDED.

**E6 - Methodological/technical papers without theoretical yield**
Paper is a methods paper (e.g., econometric technique, NLP method for patent
classification) without theoretical content relevant to the research question.

**E7 - Commentary, editorial, or opinion without original contribution**
Exclude short commentaries, practitioner editorials, and popular press pieces.
Keep review articles, research notes with conceptual content, and conference
proceedings with full empirical or theoretical papers.

**E8 - Outside time window**
Exclude papers published before 1973 (Spence's foundational paper). Optional:
consider a 2000 cutoff for empirical studies on VC signalling, since the modern
entrepreneurial signalling literature largely consolidates from around this point
- but keep pre-2000 foundational and theoretical papers.

**E9 - Language**
Exclude non-English publications.
