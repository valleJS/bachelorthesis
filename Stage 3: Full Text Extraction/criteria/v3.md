Signal vehicle definition:
In signaling theory, a **signal vehicle** describes the specific, observable
mechanism or artifact through which an underlying, unobservable quality is
communicated to an observer. For example, a firm might utilize a patent, a
public GitHub repository, or a proprietary software framework as a concrete
signal vehicle.

It is critical to distinguish a signal vehicle from a **signal category** and
a **signal message**. While a signal message denotes the underlying meaning
being communicated (such as a claim of technical competence), and a signal
category represents a higher-level classification of related signals (such as
"signals of technological capabilities"), the signal vehicle is the distinct
entity that operationalizes the communication.

Crucially, a single signal vehicle can belong to multiple signal categories
depending on the observer's context. For instance, a functional product
prototype and a public source code repository are both specific signal
vehicles. They can simultaneously belong to the category of "signals of
technological capabilities" and the category of "signals of technological
feasibility." This distinguishes them from a patent, which operates as a
signal vehicle primarily associated with the category of "technological
appropriability." Defining the specific signal vehicle is therefore essential
for analyzing how precise mechanism choices influence the transmission of
information.
